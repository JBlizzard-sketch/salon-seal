Salon and barbershop no-show deposit system


You are building a full production-grade no-show prevention and booking deposit system specifically designed for Nairobi salons, barbershops, and beauty businesses. Read the full brief, expand the vision, present ~20 phased plan, wait for "next."
The pain:
A salon in Kilimani books 20 appointments on a Saturday. Six don't show. The stylist sits idle, the slot is lost, the revenue is gone. This is not occasional — it is every single day across thousands of salons in Nairobi. The current "solution" is an angry WhatsApp message to the client, which achieves nothing. Salons have no leverage because they took no deposit, have no system, and can't enforce anything. The root cause is zero friction at booking time — a client makes an appointment with the effort of a WhatsApp message and cancels (or just doesn't come) with equal ease. The fix is a small Mpesa deposit at booking time that is forfeited on no-show. This is understood and accepted in other contexts — it just hasn't been productised cleanly for this market.
What you are building:
A platform where any salon or barbershop gets a personalised booking link. Their clients click the link, pick a service and time, pay a Ksh 200–500 deposit via Mpesa STK push, and get a confirmation. The salon sees all bookings in a clean dashboard. 2 hours before the appointment, the client gets an automatic WhatsApp reminder. If they cancel with enough notice, the deposit is refunded. If they no-show, the salon keeps the deposit. The platform takes 2.5% of each deposit transaction. Zero installation. Works on any phone. The salon's entire interaction with the system can be through WhatsApp if they prefer.
Critical nuances:
The booking link must work perfectly on low-end Android phones — this is a Nairobi salon market, not a Silicon Valley spa. Mpesa STK push is non-negotiable as the payment method — card is irrelevant here. The cancellation window (how far in advance a client can cancel for a refund) must be configurable per salon. The salon dashboard must be usable by someone with basic smartphone literacy — it cannot be complex. Deposit amounts should be configurable per service. The platform should handle the Mpesa refund flow automatically when a valid cancellation happens. Over time, the platform builds a client database for each salon — repeat clients, visit history, spending patterns — which becomes a CRM the salon never had.
Expand on this. Think about: automated post-visit review requests sent by WhatsApp; a salon "loyalty points" system built on top of the booking data; bulk SMS marketing to past clients ("we have a slot tomorrow, 20% off"); a staff scheduling view for the salon manager; analytics on peak booking days and most popular services; a "waitlist" feature for fully booked slots; and a network referral feature where a client who refers three friends gets a free treatment. This should grow into the operating system for a Nairobi salon, not just a deposit tool.
Hosting: Next.js on Vercel. Mpesa Daraja on a lightweight Railway backend. WhatsApp Business API for reminders. Minimal infrastructure — this must be cheap to run at scale given low per-transaction revenue.
